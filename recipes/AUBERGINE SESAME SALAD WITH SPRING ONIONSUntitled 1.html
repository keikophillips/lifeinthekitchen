AUBERGINE SESAME SALAD WITH SPRING ONIONS

A recipe from a Waitrose magazine approximately 25 years ago…..

Ingredients:

3 aubergines

4 tbsp toasted sesame oil

4 tbsp soy sauce

1 ½ tbsp vinegar (cider)

3 tbsp honey

3 tbsp sesame seeds

150g fine noodles (Chinese)

1 tsp finely chopped ginger

2cloves of garlic, minced

225g baby leaf spinach

1 red pepper, very finely sliced

1 bunch spring onions, sliced – including green parts

250g bean sprouts

2 handfuls of coriander leaves, chopped

1-2 tsp sriracha or other chilli sauce

Method:

Preheat the oven to hot. Prick each aubergine all over with a sharp knife or fork, then bake for 30 minutes, or until very soft. Then remove and leave them to cool.

Meanwhile, mix the oil, soy sauce, vinegar, honey. Toast the sesame seeds in either the oven or a dry pan till golden and add to this mixture. Divide the mixture in half. Add the cooked noodles to one of these halves of dressing.

Mix the garlic and ginger into the other half of the dressing. Peel the aubergines and tear the flesh into long strips. Mix into the bowl with the remaining garlicky dressing. Drop the spinach into a pot of boiling water, return it to the boil, then drain the spinach and cool it under cold water. Squeeze it dry then mix it into the aubergines, along with the finely sliced red pepper, the spring onions and the bean sprouts. Finally add the noodle mixture, season with a little black pepper and the chilli sauce. Mix in the chopped coriander, and serve.
