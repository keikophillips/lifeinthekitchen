KNOCKOUT CHOCOLATE BROWNIES

Previously, I wasn’t a fan of chocolate brownies. Butterscotch brownies filled the bill as far as I was concerned. But a recent trip to New York City to visit my eldest daughter has changed my mind completely. She served the following recipe up, and like everyone else present, I couldn’t wait to get the recipe. As you will see, when you examine the ingredients, they are massively rich in chocolate. Please use decent chocolate, with at least 60% cocoa butter content! But, they are really easy and quick to make, and even though the original recipe called for massive slices, smaller and daintier slices seem fine to me.

Ingredients: 230g dark chocolate, 140g of it chopped or hammered into small chunks or shards; 125g butter; 250g sugar; 1 tsp vanilla essence; 90g plain flour; 2 eggs, at room temperature; a pinch of salt if you use unsalted butter. Use a 20cm (8inch) square baking tin or dish, lined with Bakewell paper.

Method: Melt the butter and 90g of the chocolate together- either in a microwave or a bowl set well above simmering water, Stir to blend, then remove from the heat. Mix together, in a separate bowl, the eggs, sugar and flour. Then blend them with the butter-chocolate mixture, stir in the vanilla essence, and last of all, the remaining chocolate chunks. Put this into the baking tin or dish, and bake it in a moderately hot oven (180C or 350F) for 10-12 minutes – an inserted toothpick should come out clean. Transfer the baking tin to a rack and leave to cool before slicing.

-Carla Phillips
