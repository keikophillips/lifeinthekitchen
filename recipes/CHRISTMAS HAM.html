CHRISTMAS HAM

A really terrific recipe- not just for Christmas!

Ingredients: 1 large boneless ham – 2kg at least

3tbsp black treacle or molasses

pinch of mace or small blade of cinnamon

1 tsp whole allspice

1-2 bay leaves

1 tbsp black peppercorns

zest of ½ an orange

1 tsp cloves

juice of the orange half

For the glaze:

Handful of cloves

5 tbsp dark brown sugar

1 and ½ tbsp of mustard powder

grated zest of the other ½ orange

20 ml ginger wine

Method: Put the ham into a large stockpot and cover with cold water. Add the molasses, spices bay leaves and the zest of the orange, and the peppercorns. Bring it slowly to a simmer, skimming off any scum. Simmer gently for about an hour and a half. Using a meat thermometer, check the internal temperature. It should reach 68c.

Heat the oven to 220c-- very hot. Lift the ham out of the liquid and allow it to cool slightly, then carefully cut off the skin, leaving as much fat as possible. Score this in a diamond pattern, then stud the centres with the cloves, Put the ham into a foil lined roasting tin.

Mix the glaze ingredients into a thick paste and brush this all over the fat. Place this into the hot oven for about 25 minutes, basting it a couple of times, You can sprinkle a bit more sugar over it. The glaze should be caramelized and bubbling.

Allow it to cool completely before chilling. Leave to cool for several hours, - indeed, overnight- before serving.
