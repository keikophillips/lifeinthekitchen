EASY FRUIT CRISP

This simple pudding is so adaptable and easy to make that I’m surprised I haven’t come across it more often. It’s fine using blueberries, summer soft fruits or, at this time of the year, apples and blackberries.

It’s best made in the bowls you’re going to eat it in- use a smallish sort of ceareal or small soup bowl which is ovenproof.

For each single portion of fruit you need a teaspoon of sugar, a drop or two of lemon juice and a tiny pinch of cornflour. Mix these with the prepared fruit {chunks of peeled apple with some blackberries, say). Distribute it into the bowl(s). Add a dessertspoon of water per portion.

For each portion of topping you need 20 grams of softened butter and 20 grams of light brown sugar. If you like, you can also add a drop or two of vanilla essence. Cream these together. You can use a mixer, wooden spoon, or if it’s for one or two you can simply use your clean and warm hand. Into this stir 30 grams of self-raising flour. Now, pat this mixture into disks (one for each portion) an d place it in the bowl on top of the sides, sealing it in on the sides.

Bake in a hot oven for 10-15 minutes, until it’s slightly more browned and the fruit is starting to bubble up well. Lovely served hot, with cream, but nice when cool as well.

-Carla Phillips
