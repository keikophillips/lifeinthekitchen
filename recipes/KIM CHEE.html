KIM CHEE

This will make a fair amount- 4-6 jars, say, of the famous Korean cabbage pickle. It requires an overnight soak in a large bowl, but very little time or work otherwise. If you can, try to get hold of the wonderful Korean chilli powder. Oriental supermarkets stock it, and it has a superb aroma and taste, which will have you using it in all sorts of other recipes. Otherwise, 4 or 5 red chillies finely chopped will do.

Ingredients: Approximately 2-2and ½ kg Chinese leaves- say 2 Chinese cabbages; ½ teacup salt (non-iodized); 4 spring onions; 3 tablespoons grated fresh ginger; 3 tablespoons finely chopped or grated garlic; 2 tablespoons Korean chilli powder (or 4-5 red chillies, finely chopped; ½ grated mouli (Japanese radish) 1 tablespoon sugar; 1 tablespoon soy sauce (light).

Method: Quarter and then slice the cabbages, not too finely. In a large bowl (stainless steel, enamel, glass, plastic) toss the leaves with the salt and then cover them with water. Weigh it down slightly and then leave overnight, at room temperature.

Next day, drain, but don’t rinse. Toss with the remaining ingredients and then pack it tightly into clean glass jars. Cover them.

Leave these jars at room temperature for 2-4 days. Open the jars once or twice each day, and poke around with a skewer or sharp knife to release gasses. It will mellow in flavour.

Then, store in a fridge or cool place. The Kim Chee will last for months, though it will strengthen in flavours.

- Carla Phillips
