ONION SOUP WITH APPLES, CIDER OR PERRY

This soup is a very English version of a French favourite, complete with the bread and cheese topping. Very local ingredients enhance it; cider or perry from Whin Hill, cheese from Mrs Temple. Apples are plentiful, the rest of the ingredients easy to find – so have a go!

Ingredients: 30g butter; 700g onions, quartered and sliced very thinly; 3-4 cloves of garlic, chopped; 2 cooking apples, peeled, cored, chopped; 1 heaping tablespoon of dark brown sugar; 1 tablespoon of cider vinegar; 400ml dry cider or perry; 100ml light soy sauce; 800ml vegetable stock; salt and pepper; slivered cheddar or ‘Alpine’ cheese; sliced and toasted bread – any sort.

Method: In a large saucepan, cook the onions together with the butter for a few minutes, and then add the garlic. Lightly dust these ingredients with salt, then add the chopped apples, and cover, reducing the heat. Cook until the onions are very soft, stirring occasionally, for about 20 minutes. Then raise the heat and stir in the brown sugar, cooking until the mixture is a caramel brown colour. Stir in the vinegar.

Now, reduce the heat and stir in the cider or perry, the soy sauce and the stock. Cover the pan, and simmer for abother 20 minutes. Season with pepper (and, if you think it is necessary, salt.

Toast the bread slices, and pile slivers of cheese onto them. Heat aa overhead grill, or turn up your oven to a high temperature, Place the soup in bowls or large mugs, then place a slice of toast with the cheese atop these, Place the bowls on Bking tray, and grill or bake at high temperature until the cheese has melted and just started to bubble. Serve at once.

-Carla Phillips
