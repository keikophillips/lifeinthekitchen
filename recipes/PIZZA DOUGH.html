PIZZA DOUGH

This is a mainstay of my cooking. It’s a dough which lasts, when wrapped in a plastic bag, for up to 3 weeks in the fridge. It has multiple uses, beyond pizza- a reasonable dough for quiches, and rolled thinly, a terrific wrapping for sausage rolls. It is not necessary, with this dough, to grease or oil the baking tin or sheet, It’s simple to prepare, and the result is tasty.

Ingredients: 7g dried yeast (1 teaspoon); 280g strong white flour; 70g strong brown flour; 10g salt; 50ml olive oil; water and a tiny pinch of sugar.

First of all, soak the yeast in 100ml of lightly warm, slightly sweetened water. Leave this for at least 10 minutes – it should be foamy. Then mix together the flours and salt, in a large bowl. Add the yeasty water to this and then, the olive oil. Add enough water to this to make a soft and pliable dough. Knead this together until it is smooth and flexible. If you have a dough hook on a mixer or a processor with a very strong motor, you can use this, but in any case, this shouldn’t take too long.

With oiled hands form the dough into a ball, place it in a clean bowl, and leave, covered with a tea towel or sheet of cling film, undisturbed for 2-3 hours, to rise. Then, knock it back. It is ready for use, either now, or stored in a plastic bad in the fridge. When you use it, roll it very, very thinly – if you are worried that it may split, it can be rolled over a large piece of clingfilm, and then placed on a baking sheet.

If you are using this dough for sausage rolls, brush it with a little beaten egg before filling the dough with sausage meat. Seal it and coat it with a little more beaten egg, and the outside will appear nicely glossy.

The thinner the dough is rolled, the crisper and finer the result. Top with your favourite pizza toppings, and bake in a very hot oven, for 10-15 minutes.

When used after being refrigerated, leave it to reach room temperature before topping or filling….

-Carla Phillips
