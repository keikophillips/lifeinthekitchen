AN ITALIAN VARIATION

Some years ago I was part of a large crowd dining at a New York Pizzeria. While we all studied the menu, choosing variations which would please everyone present, we were brought a large plateful of these Italian sausage rolls.

They were delicious. The filling, savoury, herbed, slightly hot. The pastry, leftover

pizza dough, not at all stodgy. When I returned home to Norfolk, I spent some time making my own version. So here it is- fine when hot with drinks or tea and fine, cold, for picnics.

Please don’t be intimidated by pastry made with yeast. Modern powdered yeast, widely available, makes this all an easy and trouble free procedure.

Ingredients: 225g strong flour; 75g wholemeal flour; 1 teaspoon salt; 1 packet instant yeast; 2 tablespoons olive oil; water.

Method: In a food processor, combine the flours, salt and yeast. Add the olive oil, then while whizzing it add enough water to form into a ball of dough. Continue to mix until it’s smooth and malleable. Place this dough in a lightly oiled bowl, turning it round to grease the whole surface. Then cover it with either a damp towel or clingfilm.Leave to rise for at least an hour and a half.

For the sausage filling- Ingredients:1/2kg sausage meat; 1 teaspoon of fennel seeds; 1 clove of garlic; ½ teaspoon of oregano; 1 chilli, deveined and deseeded.

Method: Combine the ingredients in a food processor. Form into long, skinny ‘chipolata’ sized sausage shapes and leave in the fridge till you’re ready to use them.

When the pastry has doubled in size, knock it down. For ease of handling, divide it into quarters or thirds. On a cool, floured surface, roll out one of these dough sections, rolling as thinly as you can. This dough is infinitely more elastic than shortcrust or puff pastry, so be bold!

Place one of the segments of sausage on the pastry and form it into a sausage roll, ‘glueing’ it together by pressing the dough firmly. Pinch the dough at the ends together, cut it, then place with the joined edge downwards on a baking sheet. Repeat until you’ve used up all your sausage meat. If you have any dough left over at the end, you can use it for a bonus pizza- wrapped in clingfilm, the dough will be fine in the fridge for a week.

Prick the pastry all over with a fork, leave to rise for 15-20 minutes, then bake in a very hot oven for 15 minutes, until golden brown. When they’re cooked, slice into serving sized segments.
