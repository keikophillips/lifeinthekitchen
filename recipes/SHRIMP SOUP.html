SHRIMP SOUP

The little seaside stalls along our coast feature local delicacies: crabs, lobsters, little cupfuls of washed cockles, musses, whelks and winkles, brown and white shrimp.

I particularly like the small shrimp. They’re landed locally, fished off the Wash, boiled at sea in seawater with additional salt. This gives them their salty but satisfying savour.

Of course, it’s nice to eat them as is – breaking off the heads, then rapidly peeling off their shells. If there’s a fine black line of roe lining them, this is easily wiped off. I know it’s a fiddly job.But these little shrimp are the business as far as taste is concerned.

Now, if you’re willing to by a cupful without devouring them on the spot, try using a saucepan as a receptacle for their leavings. Then, you'’e well on your way to preparing a soup fit for the Gods.

Once you have your saucepan of shrimp debris, and have refrigerated the edible fleshy bits, add to your saucepan a chopped up carrot, onion, celery blade, parsley stalk and a few peppercorns, along with a couple of teaspoons of tomato puree and a full glass of white wine. Any sort of white wine, and in a pinch, dry fine cider would do. Last of all, cover well with water (about 3 inches to spare on top of the solids). Bring to the boil, the reduce to a simmer (or leave in a gentle oven) for slightly longer than an hour.

Next, strain the stock through a fine sieve. If you like you can chill and freeze the stock for a super sauce for a fish dish or pie. But for my soup, add a couple of tablespoons of Marsala, Sweet Sherry or Madeira, then bring back to the boil. Last of all add a couple of tablespoons of cream – any sort – and add the peeled shrimp.

Serve with warm buttery rolls and you’ll never want for anything more refined!

-Carla Phillips
