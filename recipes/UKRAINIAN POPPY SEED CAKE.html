pUKRAINIAN POPPY SEED CAKE

Originally from ‘The Moosewood C ookbook’, This has been ‘translated’ into metric measurements.

Ingredients: 100g poppy seeds; 200ml milk; 225g butter (at room temperature); 130g light brown sugar; 3 eggs; 300g flour, sifted with 1 tblsp baking powder and a tsp of baking soda [bicarb] and ½ tsp salt; 1 tsp vanilla extract; 3 tblsp lemon juice [juice of a lemon]; 1 tsp grated lemon zest – and a 20cm (8 inch) deep baking tin, greased and lined with Bakewell or greasproof paper.

Method: place the poppy seeds and milk in a small saucepan. Heat, just until the milk is starting to boil [scalding], then remove it from the heat and allow it to cool for at least a quarter of an hour. Cream the butter and sugar well, then add the eggs, one by one, Then alternatively add the sifted dried ingredients with the poppy seed-milk mixture, finishing with the dried ingredients. Stir just enough to blend well, then add the vanilla, lemon juice and zest. Spread this batter into the baking tin and bake in a moderately hot oven(350’) for 40 minutes. Cool for 10 minutes before turning out.
